Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

This project analyzes rider behavior for Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share company, using historical ride data. The goal was to understand how casual riders and annual members use the service differently and to recommend strategies to convert more casual riders into members.

Steps I Took

  • Cleaned and merged multiple CSV files using R and Excel.
  • Created new fields such as ride length and day of week to compare usage patterns.
  • Summarized trends by rider type, time of day, and day of week.
  • Built visualizations to compare casual vs. member rider behavior.

Key Insights

  • Casual riders use bikes more often on weekends and for longer rides.
  • Members ride more during weekday commuting hours with shorter, frequent trips.
  • Time-of-day and day-of-week patterns differ significantly by rider type.

Tools Used

R, Excel, data wrangling, descriptive statistics, visualization.

Deliverables

  • Cleaned datasets comparing rider behavior.
  • Visual dashboards in Tableau showing trends.
  • Recommendations for converting casual riders to annual members.
