Cyclistic Bike-Share Case Study

This project analyzes rider behavior for Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share company, using historical ride data. The goal was to understand how casual riders and annual members use the service differently and to recommend strategies to convert more casual riders into members.

Steps I Took

  • Cleaned and merged multiple CSV files in R and Excel (fixed data types, removed duplicates, handled nulls).
  • Created new fields such as ride length and day of week to compare usage patterns.
  • Summarized trends by rider type, time of day, and day of week.
  • Built visualizations to highlight differences between casual riders and annual members.

Key Insights

  • Casual riders used bikes more on weekends and for longer rides, especially during warmer months.
  • Members rode more during weekday commuting hours with shorter, frequent trips.
  • Popular stations and times differed by rider type, suggesting targeted marketing opportunities.

Tools Used

R, Excel, data wrangling, descriptive statistics, and data visualization.

Deliverables

  • Cleaned datasets and summary tables comparing casual vs. member usage.
  • Visuals showing trends by rider type, weekday, and ride length.
  • Recommendations to encourage casual riders to purchase memberships.
