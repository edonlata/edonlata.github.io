NCAA Basketball Trends & Team Insights

This project looks at NCAA basketball data to understand scoring trends, efficiency, and team performance. The goal was to practice working with sports data and highlight patterns that could matter for coaching or scouting.

Steps I Took

  • Imported game and team statistics into Excel and cleaned column names and data types.
  • Created calculated fields such as points per game, field goal percentage, and win percentage.
  • Built pivot tables to compare teams by conference, season, and offensive/defensive metrics.
  • Used charts to visualize relationships between scoring, shooting efficiency, and wins.

Key Insights

  • Teams with higher effective field goal percentages generally had stronger win records.
  • Balanced teams (solid offense and defense) performed better than teams relying only on high scoring.
  • Free throws and turnovers often made the difference in close games.
  • Conference strength and schedule difficulty played a role in overall performance.

Tools Used

Excel, pivot tables, calculated fields, and sports analytics concepts.

Deliverables

  • Cleaned NCAA team and game statistics tables.
  • Pivot tables summarizing performance by team and conference.
  • Charts showing how shooting efficiency and defense relate to winning.
  • Presentation summarizing key takeaways for non-technical audiences.
